Brithwyn is [[Geography/Calaria/index|Calaria's]] greatest mistake and Calaria's greatest competitor. A little over forty years ago, the Calarian Prime Minister granted a northern province some pseudo-autonomy after capitulating to local government demands. Then, the Prime Minister was assassinated. That northern province, Brithwyn, took advantage of the chaos to declare complete emancipation from the rest of the country. After a short but bloody series of exchanges, the council of Calaria decided to cut their losses and recognized Brithwyn as independent.

Life in Brithwyn is very similar to that of Calaria -- worship of the [[Iridescent Host|Host]] is still the predominant religion, and the general technological level is identical to that of their sister nation. The most significant difference, and the reason for the separation in the first place, is their more hardline stance against [[Org/index|Orgs]]. Brithwyn limits how deep into the country most Orgs can integrate themselves. Instead, they've reinstated the nobility that Calaria had done away with, offering title and capital as incentives to manage territory and public works. The Regent Administrator leads Brithwyn, but the nobles manage the day-to-day under him.
