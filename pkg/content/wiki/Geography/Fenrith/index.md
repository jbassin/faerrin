Fenrith is an empty land with a bustling sea. The Termean Veinlands, which covers more than 85% of the Fenrith territory, is exceptionally soft-grounded, blazing hot tundra. The ground is unsuitable for growing crops or constructing buildings, and few permanent settlements call it home. Instead, the natives of Fenrith have mostly fled to the long western coast, relying on the bounty of food and trade on the edge of their country. The Fenrithi built cities on the shoreline, but lack of space forced construction closer and closer to the ocean until the present day -- new buildings are built in the water directly.

Nowadays, most Fenrithi live in giant, reinforced glass domes under the surface. Their ships travel completely submerged, unimpeded by traffic on the ocean top, and they farm and ranch animals native to the ocean for sustenance. Their understanding of the ocean's currents has aided Fenrith in becoming a mercantile powerhouse: most large cargo companies are operated by, or with, the Fenrithi people.

Fenrith is ruled by the Fisher-Kings, a group of the four heads of the largest cargo companies. They rule over their cities but come together when making decisions that impact their people. By nature of their location, most Fenrithi ply their trade in some form on the sea -- in shipping, fishing, marine biology, meteorology, aquamancy, and others. Due to the worldliness of their trade, there's no one state religion in Fenrith. [[The Compelled]] likely has the most considerable following, but all faiths maintain a foothold here.

> [!note] Inhabitants of the Veinlands
> While most Fenrithi live in their dome-cities on the coast, some live in the Termean Veinlands. Most who live there do so to stay away from prying eyes -- fugitives from the law, so-called sovereign citizens, and self-supporting homesteaders all eke an out existence to avoid prying eyes. Crossing the Veinlands can be difficult, so most use modified automobiles adapted to run across the soft sand.
