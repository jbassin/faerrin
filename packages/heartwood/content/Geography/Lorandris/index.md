Depending on who you ask, Lorandris is either a villainous hive of murderous, pillaging scum or it's the last true bastion of freedom in an increasingly restricted world. They'd both be right. Lorandris is a *very* loose alliance between a few extra-continental enclaves -- people "removed" from the rest of Edan proper who have formed new settlements on the Fūlwinter coast.

In theory, Lorandris operates entirely off trade with the other countries, providing ships and bulk transport where [[Geography/Fenrith/index|Fenrith]] might not. In practice, Lorandris' biggest economic center is piracy. Raiding parties of Lorandrin pirates trawl the coasts for undefended port towns and capture improperly defended cargo vessels at sea. With the advent of airframes, pirate crews have taken to raiding inland -- massive ships called skybreakers land, pillage, and return to the skies before the local governments can catch up.

Truthfully, for as perfectly as they've honed their craft, Lorandris is a small country. If the others on Edan really wanted to be rid of their piracy, most any other country could swiftly topple Lorandris. The only reason the country still stands is because the others need them. Or, more accurately, they need their shipyards. Lorandrin ships are faster, can carry more cargo, can take way more abuse, and, in some cases, can turn invisible. Simply, no other country has the shipbuilding know-how to best a Lorandrin vessel, so every country buys from them.

Natives to Lorandris all work building, sailing, or sinking ships. Mere Lorandrin citizenship is a felony in other countries, so when traveling in different countries, a Lorandrin will likely deflect their country of origin.
